My Work Experience.

During my time in the engineering program, I have met a number of industry professionals through workplace field trips and guest speakers. Below I have documented several of these experiences.

Stanford & Computer History Museum

Field Trip

We visited the Stanford campus and toured the entire school. I got to learn a lot about the culture at Stanford and its great computer science program. Later we went to the Computer History Musuem were I learned about the artifacts of the information age and explored the computing revolution and its impact on society.

Patriot Jet Foundation

Field Trip

I attended the Patriot Jet Foundation where our class had a tour on Jet Aircraft and Presentation on New Technologies in Flight. First, we investigated the Polish MIG-17F where we learned a lot of important techniques aircrafts use to sustain itself on the air. For example, I learned about the speed stem and how it helped the aircraft measure the speed. Overall this experience was very memorable for me because I learned a lot of new things which I was able to implement in my POE class.

LLNL IT Job Shadow

Field Trip

I also participated in the LLNL IT Job shadow which was an amazing experience since it opened my eyes to how vast engineering can be. There are so many different jobs from the software side to hands-on engineering. I also got to chance to tour the national ignition facility laser which was a cool experience. Overall, this was definitely an eye-opener for all the differents fields in engineering and STEM in general.

Roche Career Trip

Field Trip

Due to COVID-19 this field trip was conducted virtually yet I found this to be one of the most insightful experiences. There was a panel of scientists who developed the diagnostic test and shared their journey with us. It was inspiring to see how they pivoted around the challenges of COVID-19 and adapted to make their product.

Kenny Lau

Guest Speaker

Kenny Lau is a student at Cal Poly Slo currently. My main take away from his talk was to remain proactive. As he mentioned towards the end, school is what you make of it. Even in high school he participated in a lot of entrepreneurship competitions and it later helped him at his startup accelerated program. Even in college Kenny sought after opportunities by competing in a lot of hackathons. He even placed second in his first hackathon and learned more about block chain. He later won the competition with his idea of sending cryptocurrency through text. Clearly, his efforts paid out in the end as he got a lot of publicity and mentorship from the people at Ripple. But even after he reached a great milestone he continued to work on his app and expand it for more people internationally

Laura Kegelmeyer

Guest Speaker

One guest speaker who had a major effect on me was Laura Kegelmeyer from the National Ignition Facility. She shared her intriguing experience about how she finally decided on working at Lawrence Livermore after attending various jobs. She explained how the largest laser in the world was stored in Livermore for research purposes. She also explained the impact this has on engineers and the infinite potential it has. Laura’s talk exposed me to several disciplines which I have never even thought about it.

TriValley Manufacturing day

Field Trip

I got the chance to vist local businesses and see the production behind-the-scenes. I later attended a networking lunch and met a lot of industry veterans. I then met local business and government leaders and other students where I got a chance to discover more about career paths and future career opportunities.

Chevron Career day

Field Trip

This was a webinar where several Chevron employees shared their work experience at Chevron and engineering in general. I particular found a drilling engineer, Chris Foret, to be the most interesting as I enjoyed listening to his experiences planning and executing the drilling of deepwater wells in the Gulf of Mexico.

Dublin High Engineering Academy’s Mentorship Program

When I partcipated in the mentorship program as a junior in high school I got to met Sridhar Kuppuswamy, a former employee at Apple. He explained his love for electronics and engineering in general. His upbringing was particularly inspirational to me as he wasn't fortunate like me and didn't have many engineering opportunities when he lived in India. He also gave me life advice and how he was able to make the most out of college. Overall, the mentorship program was an amazing experience and I am glad I was lucky enough to be a part of it.

Malware Detection Circuit

DE 12/04/20

Millions of people are affected by different types of malware each year, placing their information at risk. It can be confusing for people to identify which types of malware exists on their devices while knowing what to do about it. For this project, my team and I designed, simulated, and breadboarded a circuit that will identify the types of malware present on a device based on certain characteristics. With these outputs, an appropriate method of eliminating the malware will also be outputted. I was responsible for creating a portion of the circuit which dealt with a specific type of malware called the "worm." I made a circuit using a circuit design software called Multisim. After making it on multisim I made the circuit on a breadboard. For further information please refer to our documentation here.
